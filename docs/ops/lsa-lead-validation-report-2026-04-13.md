## Executive Summary

Kelly's LSA campaign received **79 leads** in 2026 YTD. Of those, **28 (47% of chargeable phone leads)** matched to real jobs in the ServiceDesk CRM. The remaining 32 phone leads were charged by Google but have no CRM record, indicating either lost conversion opportunities, calls that went to voicemail, or spam.

**Estimated revenue:** $5,000–$11,000+ from matched jobs on ~$320 LSA spend (**15x–35x ROI**).

**Critical findings:**
1. **No one is actively managing LSA leads.** 45 of 50 recent leads are unmanaged ("Active" status), messages go unread, and no leads are being graded. This is hurting both conversion rates AND LSA ranking.
2. **Four pending LSA jobs are 5-8 weeks old and still not scheduled:**

## Data Methodology

### Match Process
1. LSA export → 219 total leads, filtered to 79 in 2026
2. Normalize phone numbers (strip to last 10 digits)
3. Build CRM phone index from SDtype6 TelNmbr1/2/3 fields (1,235 unique numbers)
4. Match LSA phone → CRM customer record
5. Flag messages, in-review, and not-charged leads separately

### Match Rate
- **40% match rate** on chargeable phone leads
- Known limitation: CRM export date is March 11, 2026 with recent adds through mid-April. April leads partially covered.

## 🚨 Critical Issues

1. **Stalled jobs stuck in "Working to Schedule":** 4 LSA-sourced jobs are 5-8 weeks old and still not scheduled. These customers have almost certainly called someone else by now. Recovery opportunity:
   - Sue Cooper (Novato, Samsung fridge) — 8 weeks
   - Bob Davis (Cotati, Frigidaire laundry center) — 7 weeks
   - Lori Morada (Petaluma, GE range) — 6 weeks
   - Hugh Smith (Novato, Whirlpool fridge) — 5 weeks
2. **No active lead management.** Leads sit in "Active" status for weeks without being graded, booked, or dispositioned. Google penalizes this.
3. **Messages go unread.** Response time is a ranking factor.
4. **No lead grading feedback to Google.** Automated credit system works, but manual grading improves future lead quality.
5. **No post-call follow-up on unmatched leads.** 32 charged leads with no CRM record = no closure on what happened to each call.
6. **B2B leads not tagged.** Commercial jobs like Roxanne Liu Family Dental need different handling and have different revenue profiles.
7. **Appliance category mis-tagging.** Google showed one lead as "Microwave" that was actually a GE Washer. Don't trust LSA job type.

## 📋 Improvement Plan — The LSA Management SOP

### Daily (5 min, for Mark/Office Staff)
1. Open LSA dashboard first thing each morning
2. Review new leads from the previous 24 hours
3. Respond to any unread messages within 2 hours of receipt
4. Note any obvious spam/wrong-service and mark for grading

### Weekly (15 min, Shane/Ideas Realized or office)
1. Pull fresh LSA leads export (DOWNLOAD button)
2. Cross-reference new leads against CRM Type6 for past week
3. For matched leads: mark as BOOKED in LSA dashboard
4. For unmatched charged leads: listen to SCS recording, determine why (no answer, no book, wrong service, spam)
5. Grade all leads in LSA: Thumbs up (booked + completed), Thumbs down with reason (spam, wrong service, out of area)
6. Document weekly summary in tracking sheet

### Monthly (30 min, Shane)
1. Pull fresh CRM Type6 export
2. Run full batch match (like today's analysis)
3. Calculate actual revenue per matched lead (join Type6 to SalesByMore)
4. Calculate ROI (revenue / LSA spend)
5. Identify trends: appliance mix, city mix, lost-lead patterns
6. Update `docs/ops/platforms/google-lsa.md` with monthly metrics
7. Archive report as `lsa-lead-validation-report-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

### Quarterly (Shane)
1. Full year-to-date analysis like this report
2. Compare to previous quarters
3. Identify optimization opportunities (bid adjustments, service area changes, budget shifts)
4. Review whether LSA budget should increase based on ROI

## 🔄 Applying This Methodology to Other Platforms

This same validation chain will work for:

### Google Ads Search Campaign
- Pull lead/conversion data via Google Ads MCP
- Match gclid/click date + form submission to CRM phone numbers
- Calculate conversion revenue

### Thumbtack
- Pull lead data from Thumbtack dashboard (manual export or API if available)
- Match phone numbers to CRM
- Compare CPL and CPA vs LSA

### Yelp
- Pull contact/request data from Yelp dashboard
- Match phone numbers to CRM
- Calculate ROI vs free Yelp profile value

### Nextdoor
- Track recommendations and DMs
- Match to eventual jobs via CRM
- Measure referral value

### Facebook/Instagram (when live)
- Track form submissions and Messenger leads
- Match to CRM
- Compare to LSA/Google Ads performance

**The common thread:** every lead source generates a phone number or name. The CRM has that same phone number or name. Matching them produces the ROI picture that none of these platforms can give you alone.
