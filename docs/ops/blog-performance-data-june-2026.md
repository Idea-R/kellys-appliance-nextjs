## Key takeaways for the next batch

### 1. The dryer post is the single biggest opportunity on the whole blog
37,051 impressions, only 209 clicks (0.56% CTR). It ranks for a massive volume of dryer searches but barely gets clicked. Two plays:
- **Refresh its title/meta** for CTR (it's an old June 2025 post with a clinical title)
- **Write MORE dryer content** to capture the latent demand — "Dryer Not Heating," "Dryer Vent Cleaning," "Dryer Making Noise." The search volume is clearly there.

### 2. Dishwasher is nearly invisible
The only dishwasher post (`common-dishwasher-problems`) has 88 impressions, 0 clicks in 3 months. For a major service category, that's a content vacuum. Dishwasher batch is justified by the *absence* of competition from our own site, not by proven demand — but the washer/dryer/fridge categories all prove that symptom + how-to content ranks well here.

### 3. The local/community pattern keeps winning on CTR
APE post: 7.3% CTR — 10x the site blog average. The "50 Years in Sonoma County" post (just shipped) should perform similarly. Keep producing local-angle content.

### 4. "why you should call a professional" is a CTR disaster
1,715 impressions, 1 click (0.06%). It ranks but the title/snippet is repelling clicks. Candidate for a rewrite or merge.

### 5. Newer posts (frost, fridge water, right-to-repair) are still ramping
2 clicks or fewer but climbing impressions. Normal — they were published April and take 60-90 days to mature. The June batch will follow the same curve.
