## The 4 rules that make a photo usable

1. **Shoot in landscape (phone sideways) for hero shots, portrait is fine for detail/close-ups.** We can crop landscape down, but we can't un-crop a portrait.
2. **Before/after pairs must be framed the SAME way.** Same distance, same angle, same part of the machine. Stand in the same spot for the "after" as you did for the "before." This is what lets us build the slide-to-reveal sliders (like the new dryer post). If the framing drifts, the slider looks broken.
3. **Wipe the lens and get light on it.** A quick wipe and the under-counter flashlight or a work light makes a night-and-day difference. Avoid heavy shadow.
4. **Note the machine in the filename or a quick text.** Brand + type + fuel, e.g. `whirlpool-dishwasher-drain`, `samsung-dryer-gas`. We don't publish customer info, just the appliance.

> **Privacy:** Never include anything that identifies the customer (mail, photos, faces, house numbers, paperwork). Just the appliance and the part.

## How to send them

- Text or AirDrop to Shane, or drop in the shared folder.
- A quick batch every couple weeks is perfect. Even phone photos are great if they follow the 4 rules above.
- If you nail a clean before/after pair, flag it — those are gold for the slider posts.

## What Ideas Realized does with them

- Convert to web-friendly `.webp` (fast loading).
- Build slide-to-reveal before/after sliders where we have matched pairs.
- Place them in blog posts, with a caption crediting "our techs on a real Sonoma County call."
- Reuse the best ones on service pages and social.
