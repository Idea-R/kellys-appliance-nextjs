## Strategic Observations

1. **Operational efficiency is a bigger lever than ad optimization right now.** $169K in April revenue vs $1,259 in ad spend — even doubling ad effectiveness only adds maybe $1K/month. Reviving 25% of the 448 stuck jobs is potentially ~$28K. Focus operations first.

2. **Search ads are now efficient enough to scale.** $7.38 CPA on Search is excellent for this category. The Maximize Conversions algorithm has matured. Worth testing a budget bump.

3. **LSA is paying for visibility, not efficiency.** Higher CTR (17.4% vs 11.7%) confirms the Google Guaranteed badge gets clicks, but at 2.4x the cost per conversion. Consider it a brand/credibility expense, not a pure performance channel.

4. **Property managers are an underutilized channel.** GRT, Baywood Arms, and similar accounts produce volume with predictable repeat business. No marketing attribution data, but worth a deliberate B2B program.

5. **Tech-level revenue distribution is healthy but uneven.** Top 4 techs (JS, JD, BB, SF) drove $107K of $169K (63%). MK has highest avg ticket but lowest volume — could be scaled if scheduling allows.

6. **Lead-to-job attribution is the next infrastructure piece.** With OATAS in place and lead capture wired up, every monthly report from here forward could include real per-keyword ROI. Currently we have ad data + business data, but no glue between them.
