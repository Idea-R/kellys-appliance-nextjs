# Kelly's Appliance Center: Monthly Performance Report

**Reporting Period:** June 2026
**Prepared for:** Kelly's Appliance Center leadership
**Prepared by:** Ideas Realized (shane@ideas-realized.com)

---

## Executive Summary

June was a growth month across every channel we track.

| Headline | June 2026 | vs May |
|---|---|---|
| Organic clicks from Google | 923 | +44% |
| Organic impressions | 67,080 | +20% |
| Paid conversions (Search + Local Services) | 141 | efficient at ~$11 each |
| Website leads matched to real jobs | 62 | first full month tracked |

**What stands out this month:**

1. **Organic search is accelerating.** Clicks grew 44% while impressions grew 20%, which means the site is not just being seen more, it is earning a higher share of the clicks it sees. Click-through rate rose from 1.1% to 1.4%.
2. **Paid advertising is converting efficiently.** Google Search brought in leads at $7.21 each and Local Services Ads at $15.57 each, both well below the value of a typical repair job.
3. **We can now trace website leads to real jobs.** For the first time we can follow a lead from the website form to an actual invoice. 62 June jobs came from website leads, with $8,066 already booked and more still open on the schedule.
4. **The next measurement gap is the marketplaces.** Yelp and Nextdoor leads are being captured, and connecting them to completed jobs is the focus for next month.

## Organic Search (SEO)

Google organic delivered its strongest month of the year so far.

| Metric | June 2026 | May 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| Clicks | 923 | 639 |
| Impressions | 67,080 | 55,764 |
| Click-through rate | 1.4% | 1.1% |
| Average position | 11.6 | 11.3 |

The growth is broad. Branded searches (people looking for Kelly's by name) continue to convert at a high rate, and the blog content is now the top source of new organic clicks. The washing-machine repair guide alone brought in 150 clicks, and the dryer and oven guides are each drawing thousands of impressions per month.

**The biggest opportunity:** several pages earn very high impressions but a low share of clicks, which means the demand is there and the ranking is close. The dryer guide drew 12,157 impressions at a 0.8% click rate, and the oven guide drew 5,433 at 0.5%. Sharpening the titles and descriptions on these pages is a direct lever to turn existing visibility into visits. In parallel, "dishwasher repair near me" is showing 1,442 impressions but sitting at position 27, a clear signal to build dedicated dishwasher content.

## Paid Advertising

Both paid channels ran efficiently in June.

| Channel | Spend | Conversions | Cost per conversion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Search (Appliance Repair, Sonoma and Marin) | $598 | 83 | $7.21 |
| Google Local Services Ads | $903 | 58 | $15.57 |
| **Total** | **$1,501** | **141** | **~$10.65 blended** |

Google Search continues to be the most cost-effective channel, delivering leads at just over $7 each. Local Services Ads carry a higher cost per lead but deliver pre-qualified, ready-to-book customers, and at $15.57 per conversion they remain well inside the value of an average repair job.

## Qualified Leads (Attribution)

This is the section that changed the most this month. We now match website leads to real jobs automatically, so we can report on outcomes rather than just lead counts.

| Source | Leads captured (June) | Matched to a real job |
|---|---|---|
| Website form (organic and Google) | 112 | 62 |
| Yelp | 114 | capture live, matching in progress |
| Nextdoor | 16 | capture live, matching in progress |
| Property management | 6 | capture live, matching in progress |
| **Total captured** | **248** | |

Of the 62 website leads matched to jobs, 33 have already been invoiced for **$8,066 in booked revenue**, and 29 more are still open on the schedule, so the June figure will continue to climb as those jobs complete. That is a 58% match rate from website form to real job, which is strong for first-touch web leads.

Yelp and Nextdoor leads are being captured into the same system. Connecting those to completed jobs is the next step, since those platforms do not pass a phone number in a consistent format, which is what the automatic matching relies on.

## What We Delivered in June

- **Six new blog posts** published on high-demand repair topics, including dryer-not-heating, oven-not-heating, refrigerator noises, washer error codes, hot-weather appliance care, and the shop's 50-year milestone.
- **Local referral network launched** on the county service pages, connecting customers with trusted local partners across Sonoma, Marin, and Napa.
- **Preferred-contact-method field** added to every website form, so customers are reached the way they prefer.
- **Automatic lead-to-job matching** went live, which is what makes the attribution above possible.
- **Search Console reporting restored** for reliable, direct organic data going forward.

## Where It's Headed

If organic impressions keep growing at their current pace and we lift click-through on the high-impression pages toward 2%, organic clicks are on track to reach roughly 1,100 to 1,200 next month. Paid is expected to hold steady at its current efficient cost per lead. The attribution figure will grow as this month's open jobs close and as marketplace matching comes online.

These are conservative projections based on current trends, not guarantees.
