## The one-line picture

The site was shown ~55,800 times in May and clicked ~639 times (1.1% CTR). The growth lever is not more impressions, it is converting the ones we already earn. The live page data makes the shape obvious, and it is different from what the volume alone suggested: **the biggest wins are blog posts that already rank on page 1 but have a weak title, so they get skipped.** Three problems, three fixes:

- **Lever 1, CTR-up (biggest + most certain):** pages already on page 1 with a sub-1% CTR. Fix = rewrite title + meta. No ranking work.
- **Lever 2, rank-up:** pages with real impressions stuck on page 2-3. Fix = strengthen the page and interlink so it climbs.
- **Lever 3, cleanup:** a large block of AC / HVAC impressions we rank for but should not. Dead weight on the average position.

## Lever 3: the AC / HVAC impression leak (new finding from live data)

The site is being shown for a large block of **air-conditioning queries it should not rank for at all**: "ac repair sebastopol," "ac maintenance mill valley," "ac installation napa," "ac repair services petaluma," and dozens more, almost all at deep positions (position 20 to 75) with zero clicks. Kelly's does not do AC. These are hundreds of irrelevant impressions a month that earn nothing and drag down the average position metric. Action: find where the city pages mention HVAC/AC (or why Google associates them) and trim it, so the location pages are clearly appliance-repair only. This sharpens relevance and the position average.

Still the clearest signal across both channels.
