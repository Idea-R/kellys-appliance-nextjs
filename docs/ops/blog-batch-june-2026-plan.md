### Two distinct winning patterns

1. **Volume play (~250 clicks/mo):** Numbered listicle, symptom-based title, broad appeal — e.g., "Signs Your X Needs Repair Before It Breaks Down"
   - Tradeoff: ~1.9% CTR (decent), big impression base
2. **Local/community play (~26 clicks/mo, 4.7% CTR):** Community story, named local landmark, neighbor-to-neighbor voice
   - Tradeoff: smaller search volume, BUT 2.5× the CTR — these posts also build trust + backlinks

**Implication:** Best batch is a mix — 2-3 symptom-volume posts + 1-2 local/history posts.

## Style elements that work

From reading the top performers (washing-machine signs + APE closing):

1. **Conversational openers** — first paragraph speaks directly to the reader's situation, not "In today's world..." filler
2. **Numbered H2 sections** — 5-7 numbered items work for symptom listicles
3. **Embedded images** — the washing machine post has 0 inline images; APE post has 1 storefront photo (works because it's the local angle)
4. **Real Kelly's color** — "we make the drive from Cotati...", "Cecil and Roger built that business" — specific local references
5. **"When to call a pro" tag at the end** of each section in the troubleshooting posts — natural CTA without being pushy
6. **FAQ schema** with 4-5 questions at the bottom
7. **Internal links** to service pages and other blog posts in the body
8. **CTA buttons at end** linking to `/schedule-prep` and `/services/[type]`
9. **No em dashes**, no AI tells, no "delve" / "embark" / "landscape"

## What to NOT repeat from current blog mistakes

- **Don't lead with "In today's world..."** or any landscape/embark/delve language
- **Don't end with "Don't hesitate to reach out"** — use natural close like "Give us a call" or "Book online"
- **Don't write 800 words** — they don't rank. Aim for 1,500+ on every post
- **Don't skip FAQ schema** — every top performer has it
- **Don't use em dashes** — every one we ship gets caught in humanize pass anyway
