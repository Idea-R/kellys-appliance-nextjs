# Local Press Pitch: Kelly's Appliance Center

**Goal:** earn an editorial story (and a backlink) in North Bay local media. Editorial coverage is one of the few high-authority links competitors cannot easily copy, and it reinforces the entity signals that both Google and AI answer engines use.

**Owner:** Ideas Realized (shane@ideas-realized.com), on behalf of Kelly's Appliance Center.

---

## The angle (lead with the story, not the ad)

The strongest hook is **right to repair meets a half-century family business**: a Cotati shop that still rebuilds appliance control boards by hand instead of telling people to buy new. That is timely (right-to-repair is a live news topic), genuinely local, and it separates Kelly's from the throwaway model. The 50-plus years and the Diamond Certified rating are the credibility underneath the story, not the headline.

Backup angles if a reporter wants a different frame:
- **Half a century, still family-run.** Founded 1975, owner Mark Kelly, a team that stays for decades, serving three generations of the same Sonoma County families.
- **The economics of repair vs replace.** With appliance prices up, a good repair keeps a working machine (and its materials) out of the landfill. Kelly's can speak to real numbers.
- **Local resilience.** Kelly's serviced homes through the 2017 fires and the rebuild, and knows the appliance mix of the rebuilt neighborhoods.

## The pitch email

**Subject:** A Cotati shop that still fixes what everyone else says to throw away

Hi [Reporter or Business Editor],

I wanted to flag a local business story you might find worth a look. Kelly's Appliance Center in Cotati has been repairing appliances in Sonoma, Marin, and Napa counties since 1975, and one thing sets them apart in an era of throwaway appliances: they still rebuild electronic control boards by hand rather than telling a customer their five-year-old refrigerator is a lost cause.

It is a right-to-repair story with a very local face. Owner Mark Kelly has run the shop for decades, several of his technicians have been there ten to twenty-five years, and the business is Diamond Certified, an independent quality rating earned from hundreds of verified customer surveys.

There is a good visual story here too: technicians at the bench rebuilding a board, the parts department, and a family business that has watched the county change over 50 years. Mark is happy to talk about how repair economics have shifted, what breaks most often and why, and why he bets on fixing over replacing.

Would you be interested in a conversation or a shop visit? I can arrange access, photos, and any figures you need.
